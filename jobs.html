Access to Jobs

Workforce navigation for every Missouri county — WIOA navigation, job matching, and career pathway tools empowering workforce development staff, job seekers, and career counselors.

The problem

Missouri's workforce development system is governed by complex WIOA regulations that vary in implementation across 114 counties. Job seekers struggle to identify eligible training programs, navigate On-the-Job Training funding, and understand which services are available in their specific county.

Workforce development staff and career counselors spend hours manually matching individuals to programs, interpreting eligibility requirements, and tracking fragmented resources across multiple agencies. The gap between available workforce programs and the people who need them remains stubbornly wide — especially in rural counties with fewer resources.

Our approach

Access to Jobs provides AI-powered workforce navigation tools that cover all 114 Missouri counties. Built on current WIOA program data and designed for real-world use, these tools help job seekers find eligible training, navigate OJT funding opportunities, and build career pathways tailored to their circumstances.

Every tool is open source, designed for practical workforce development, and built to work within the Claude.ai platform — no coding required, no cost to the end user.

Key Capabilities

WIOA Program Matching

Match job seekers to WIOA-eligible training programs based on their location, background, and career goals — covering all 114 Missouri counties with up-to-date program data.

OJT Funding Navigation

Navigate On-the-Job Training funding opportunities, eligibility requirements, and application processes to connect employers and job seekers with available workforce development dollars.

Career Pathway Builder

Build structured career pathways that align job seeker skills and interests with in-demand occupations, training sequences, and credential stacking opportunities in Missouri.

County-Specific Resources

Access workforce resources, job center locations, and program availability tailored to each of Missouri's 114 counties — from urban centers to rural communities.

Eligibility Screening

Screen job seekers for WIOA eligibility across multiple program categories — Adult, Dislocated Worker, Youth, and specialized populations — with clear next-step guidance.

Training Program Finder

Search and compare WIOA-approved training programs by location, occupation, cost, and completion time — helping job seekers and counselors make informed training decisions.

Who this is for

Workforce Development Staff

Program managers, case managers, and workforce board professionals who need efficient tools to match clients with WIOA-eligible programs and track outcomes across Missouri counties.

Job Seekers

Individuals looking for employment, career changes, or training opportunities who need help navigating WIOA programs, finding eligible training, and building career pathways in their county.

Career Counselors

Career advisors and counselors who guide individuals through job searches, skill assessments, and training decisions — and need structured tools to navigate Missouri's workforce programs.

Missouri Job Center Staff

Front-line staff at Missouri Job Centers who assist walk-in clients with job searches, program enrollment, and resource referrals — and need quick access to county-specific information.

How to use these tools

These are Claude Skills — structured AI prompt systems that run inside Claude.ai. To get started:

  1. Clone the repository from GitHub
  2. Upload the SKILL.md file to a Claude Project as a project knowledge file
  3. Start a conversation — the skill will guide you through its capabilities

No coding required. These tools work entirely through conversation with Claude.
